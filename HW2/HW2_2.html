Problem Set 2 - Comparing different methods¶

Data¶

The data we are going to analyse comes from EUR-Lex (http://eur-lex.europa.eu/homepage.html), a site where EU law and other public EU documents can be found. For the extration of the data we decided to take al the legislation documents from 2005 to 2015. Moreover the word "palestine" should be found in the search. The following document is going to apply count dictionary and TD-IDF to try to understand the evolution of Palestine in different aspects.

Dictionaries¶

To apply the metioned methods we extracted dictionaries from Harvard IV-4 of the following categories:

- Economy: 510 words of an economic, commercial, industrial, or business orientation, including roles,
collectivities, acts, abstract ideas, and symbols, including references to money. Includes names of common
commodities in business.
- Legal: 192 words relating to legal, judicial, or police matters.
- Militar: 88 words relating to military matters.
- Politics: 263 words having a clear political character, including political roles, collectivities, acts,
ideas, ideologies, and symbols.

Coding¶

Firstly we have created a function that computed the count and tf-idf dictionary methods. Secondly we have applied these functions to our Data. Moreover we have done this for the 4 types of diccinaries to see how different woordings can change the outputs of the ranking. The rankings obtained by each method and diccionary have being included in the data so we could see how rankings vary.

Top Documents¶

Finally we are going to check which topic is most important for each of the documents, and see how documents are distributed throught the topics. That means that for each document we are going to check which category had the higher TD-IDF score. The category that has the highest score will count as 1 document of that dictionary. As expected the economy topic is the category which the td-idf score is the highest for most of the document. Moreover we can see that military and legal category are very equal in the importance in the documents.
